What is ALYRA?

A loan against your asset, not a sale of it.

ALYRA helps clients access funds without selling valuable assets they want to keep.

Each eligible asset is assessed by a specialist, held securely for the agreed loan term, and returned once you've repaid.

No public listing. No auction. No obligation.

How it works

From confidential enquiry to asset return.

A simple, private process from first enquiry to the return of your asset.

01

Share your asset details

Submit photos, key details, and any documents you have. ALYRA reviews your enquiry privately and confirms whether the asset is suitable for assessment.

02

Receive a specialist assessment

A specialist reviews the asset's condition, documents, value, market demand, and custody requirements.

03

Review your loan offer

If approved, you receive a written offer showing the loan amount, fees, interest, repayment terms, redemption rights, and what happens if you don't repay, before you proceed.

04

Repay and retrieve your asset

Your asset is held securely during the loan term and returned once the loan is repaid under the agreed terms.

Valuation & specialist assessment

Specialist assessment for high-value assets.

High-value assets are not assessed by brand, weight, artist name, or recent sale price alone.

Condition, provenance, rarity, service history, documents, market demand, and likely resale value all matter.

ALYRA reviews each eligible asset before making a loan offer. The assessment considers both value and suitability as loan security.

Security & custody

Securely held until repayment.

When a loan is approved, your asset is documented and held securely for the agreed loan term. You remain the owner. ALYRA holds the asset only as security for the loan.

Your asset is at risk if repayment obligations are not met.

Custody process
1
Received and checked

Returned after repayment

Borrow or Sell

Keep the asset, or sell it permanently.

Borrowing may suit clients who want access to funds while keeping ownership of their asset during the loan term. Selling may suit clients who are ready to transfer ownership permanently.
